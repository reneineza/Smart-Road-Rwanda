# User Personas

To ensure SmartRoad Rwanda is user-centered and solves real engineering challenges, the MVP is designed around four primary user personas.

## 1. Transportation Engineer
**Goals:**
- Analyze road network capacity and structural health.
- Access reliable historical data for infrastructure design.
- Evaluate the impact of proposed developments on traffic flow.

**Problems:**
- Currently relies on fragmented Excel spreadsheets and outdated physical reports.
- Lacks a unified spatial view of condition data overlaid with traffic data.

**Main Actions Inside System:**
- Open the Map Explorer to visualize specific road links.
- View detailed traffic counts and calculate Level of Service (LoS).
- Review historical pavement condition data.
- Run spatial queries to identify road segments meeting specific criteria.

## 2. Government Transport Manager (e.g., RTDA Official)
**Goals:**
- Understand overall network performance at a macro level.
- Prioritize infrastructure investments and allocate maintenance budgets effectively.
- Monitor the execution and outcomes of infrastructure projects.

**Problems:**
- Struggles to justify budget requests without clear, aggregated visual data.
- Difficulty tracking overall network deterioration year-over-year.

**Main Actions Inside System:**
- Access the high-level Analytics Dashboard daily.
- Review condition summaries and safety indicators across districts.
- Identify top priority areas for maintenance interventions.
- Generate executive reports for ministerial review.

## 3. Consultant Engineer
**Goals:**
- Collect baseline data for new road design or feasibility studies.
- Prepare accurate engineering reports.
- Analyze specific localized projects (e.g., a new intersection design).

**Problems:**
- Spends excessive time requesting baseline data from government agencies.
- Data provided is often in incompatible formats or lacks spatial context.

**Main Actions Inside System:**
- Search and filter the road network for a specific project area.
- Download raw traffic and condition data for external modeling (e.g., in Civil 3D or HDM-4).
- View existing drainage and road furniture assets in the project vicinity.

## 4. Field Inspector
**Goals:**
- Efficiently collect accurate field information regarding asset conditions.
- Upload supporting evidence (photos, notes).
- Ensure data is immediately available to the engineering team in the office.

**Problems:**
- Uses paper forms that get lost, damaged, or require manual transcription.
- Difficulty accurately recording the exact GPS coordinates of a defect.

**Main Actions Inside System:**
- Use the mobile-responsive Data Collection page on site.
- Select an asset on the map to log a new inspection.
- Fill out standardized condition forms.
- Capture GPS location automatically and upload photos directly from a mobile device.
