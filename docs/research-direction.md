# Research Opportunities & Directions

SmartRoad Rwanda is not just an operational software tool; it is a platform capable of generating novel insights in Transportation Engineering and Smart City planning for developing nations.

## 1. Smart Infrastructure
Investigating the integration of low-cost IoT sensors to monitor structural health, drainage capacity, and localized flooding on road networks in real-time, enabling proactive rather than reactive responses.

## 2. AI in Transportation Engineering
Utilizing machine learning to analyze historical pavement condition data, traffic loads, and climate data to build localized predictive deterioration models. This approach improves upon generic empirical models by adapting to local environmental and operational conditions.

## 3. Digital Twins
Exploring the creation of a dynamic digital replica of the transportation network. A digital twin simulates traffic flow, public transit routing, and the impact of road closures or new infrastructure projects virtually, before they are physically built or altered.

## 4. Predictive Maintenance
Developing optimization algorithms that balance constrained municipal and national budgets against network-wide pavement preservation needs. The goal is to maximize the overall Pavement Condition Index (PCI) of the network over its lifecycle.

## 5. Sustainable Transportation Systems
Analyzing public transport utilization, non-motorized transport (NMT) infrastructure, and mode-shift behaviors to promote sustainable urban mobility, reduce congestion, and lower greenhouse gas emissions.

## 6. Transportation Data Platforms for Developing Countries
Researching software architectural patterns, AI integration, and data collection methodologies (such as mobile phone crowdsourcing or drone imagery analysis) that are effective in resource-constrained environments where traditional data collection is prohibitively expensive.
